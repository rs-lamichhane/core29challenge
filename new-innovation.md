The "Tracking" Fallacy: A 2026 study found virtually no relationship between users manually logging their mobility and actual carbon reduction. Why? Because manual logging is high-friction. Users get bored and stop.

The MaaS Adoption Barrier: 2025 research on Mobility as a Service (MaaS) shows that people choose transport based on convenience and reliability first, while sustainability is a secondary afterthought. If an app makes a user work hard to find the "green" route, they will just grab their car keys.

Temporal & Spatial Dislocation: Behavioral science shows humans cannot connect a single action today (driving to work) with a vague, delayed consequence (global warming in 10 years, or heart disease in 20 years). The threat isn't immediate or visible enough.
